Budapest

Making its home on both banks of the Danube River, Budapest is a city of cultural, intellectual and architectural beauty. Spend the morning at a cafe taking in the views across the Danube and the afternoon exploring the historic Castle Hill. With all of Budapest’s markets, ruin bars and historic landmarks, you will always find something to do.

Ljubljana

A true eye-catcher, the city of Ljubljana is as striking as they come. A pedestrian and cyclists dream, car traffic is restricted in the city centre granting peaceful strolls or cruises along the winding city streets. The heart of the city sprawls along both sides of the mysterious Ljubljanica River, allowing for warm summer nights to be spent dining al fresco at one of the many river side cafes.

Belgrade

The tumultuous meeting of the Sava and Danube rivers must have slowly worked its way into the city of Belgrade because its culture is anything less than vibrant. Clinging to historic landmarks while being transformed into a modern city, Belgrade is one of the most happening cities in Europe. Whether you want to explore the relics of the past, or join in the creative cultural movement, Belgrade has it all on offer.

Hanoi

Negotiating the streets of Hanoi by foot may seem like a death wish, but once you get the hang of dodging scooters you’ll realize it’s just the way of life. Join the locals for a Vietnamese coffee or a Bia Hoi for 25 cents, or indulge in Vietnam’s exquisite cuisine at any corner. Hanoi is raw and radiant with promises of new discoveries at every turn.

Chiang Mai

Immerse yourself in the tranquil city of Chiang Mai as you wander the backstreets of the ancient city center with its captivating Thai culture. A short drive from the city will put you in the lush hillsides of northern Thailand where you can explore rainforests, cool down under gentle waterfalls, or unwind in a natural hot spring.

Muddle together ancient cultures and western architecture, add an unexpectedly diverse food culture, and top it with a seriously skilled street art scene and you’ve got the eclectic city of George Town. One could spend weeks tasting what is some of the best food in Southeast Asia, exploring the shophouses of the Unesco World Heritage Zone, or hunting down their favorite street art dotting the city. This patchwork of culture allows for limitless discoveries.
